The 60 best gifts for college students in 2025

Today's college students are on the pulse of what's cool. They want gifts that are functional yet aesthetic, fit in their dorm room or apartment, and make their lives easier. The best gifts for college students satisfy all this and more. As you shop for the young adults in your life this year, we've rounded up a list of the best gifts for college students — from collegiate themed gifts to show off their school pride to tech upgrades and self-care products they can use everyday.

This gift guide features a sponsored inclusion from lululemon. The product also meets our editorial criteria in terms of quality and value.

A pouch that shows off their school spirit

Uncommon Goods Collegiate Pouches

Check price at Uncommon Goods

If they're all about school spirit, go for a gift that celebrates all of the little things that make their college a special place. These pouches do just that. Each pouch features a woven design that was hand-drawn with input from each school's community (alumni, fans, faculty, etc.) to ensure it accurately encompasses the essence of that place. It's a super cute way to store cash, toiletries, or any other trinkets with a touch of campus pride.

The "it" water bottle

Owala FreeSip Stainless Steel Water Bottle

Check price at Amazon
Check price at Owala

In the world of young adults, the water bottle you carry matters. Owala's FreeSip is the latest "it" water bottle. It features a built-in straw and a leak-proof cap. Our kitchen editor approves of it: you can read more in our Owala FreeSip review. If you want to get even more ahead of the curve, the next iteration of the water bottle, the FreeSwip Sway, was designed around customer feedback; it's cup-holder friendly and features a convenient carrying strap.

A spacious tote

Lululemon Daily Multi-Pocket Tote Bag

Check price at Lululemon

When they're heading to the quad carrying all their odds and ends, this big, versatile Lululemon tote will keep it together in style. Its sturdy exterior is water repellent and has a slight sheen. Multiple outer drop pockets allow easy access to their phone and water bottle, and its cavernous 20-liter main compartment can store bulky items that otherwise won't fit in their backpack.

Jeans that are secretly sweatpants

Rag & Bone Miramar Wide-Leg Pants

Check price at Bloomingdale's
Check price at Rag & Bone

For those 8 AM classes, these are a must-have. Our style editors reviewed Rag & Bone's viral sweatpant jeans and were delighted to find that they're really worth the hype. They're made with a soft terry-cloth fabric that drapes like real lightweight denim, and got so popular that Rag & Bone made a men's version, too.

A monogrammed leather shave bag

Leatherology Small Shave Bag

Check price at Leatherology

The dreaded truth of college is that you'll most likely need to schlep your shower belongings to a communal area if you live in the dorms. Grab them a leather shave bag that they'll use for years to come.

If you're looking for a chic aesthetic, Dagne Dover also makes a great neoprene toiletry bag named the Hunter (from $45) that's built to accommodate makeup. If they have a lot of toiletries, you'll probably want to get the large size for $65.

A sunrise alarm clock for a gentle, phone-free wakeup

Hatch Restore 3

Check price at Hatch

Using your phone as an alarm clock makes it a lot more tempting to start scrolling on your phone first thing in the morning, which isn't the healthiest habit. A sunrise alarm clock can help your student have a much better start to their day than immediately checking emails or scrolling on Instagram. The Hatch Restore 3 is a great sunrise alarm option with a ton of special features to help them develop a new sleep routine.

An easy-to-install bedside shelf

BedShelfie DormEssential

Check price at Amazon
Check price at BedShelfie

No tools required — this bedside shelf simply slides and clamps onto a bunk bed frame and serves as a floating nightstand. It has a phone shelf, a cup holder that can fit a 40 oz tumbler, two hooks, and bungee cords for attaching a power strip.

A nourishing lip tint

Rhode Peptide Lip Tint

Check price at Rhode

College kids are always up to date on the latest trends. Any beauty-obsessed student is sure to have heard the hype about Rhode's nourishing lip tint that moisturizes dry lips but looks like a gloss with a nice burst of pigment. Beauty contributor Julia Guerra complimented the "color payoff" and how it looks good on all skin tones in our Rhode skincare review.

A pair of cozy, vintage-look socks for the dorm halls

Bombas Vintage Stripes Half Calf Socks

Check price at Bombas

Dorm floors are notoriously cold and, frankly, a little gross for bare feet. These socks from Bombas have a vintage collegiate look and will keep your student's toes warm and clean. Associate style editor Gabrielle Chase called them "the softest socks I own" in our Bombas socks review.

Statement earrings for sensitive lobes

Ana Luisa Ash Double Earrings

Check price at Ana Luisa

Those with sensitive skin who can't usually wear chunky jewelry will appreciate these hoops — they're hypoallergenic and even comfortable enough to sleep in.

" […] I've been wearing Ana Luisa pieces for over a year with absolutely no issues. The pieces still feel great, and I've never once felt even a whiff of irritation, let alone a rash," writes style editor Samantha Crozier in her Ana Luisa jewelry review. "I'm also a fan of how lightweight the jewelry, particularly the earrings, are. Even with somewhat chunky hoops, I can forget that I'm wearing them."
