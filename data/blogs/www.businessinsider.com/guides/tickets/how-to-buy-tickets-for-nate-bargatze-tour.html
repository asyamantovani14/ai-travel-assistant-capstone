    Hit comedic star Nate Bargatze is taking his stand-up routine on the road this year for his massive 2025 Big Dumb Eyes World Tour. The tour will see the artist cover over 100 stops, spanning dozens upon dozens of cities and countries. While there is no shortage of locations to catch the laughs, tickets are in high demand, and shows are selling fast. However, there are still options for how to buy tickets to Nate Bargatze’s 2025 tour.

    Known for his “clean” comedy, usually avoiding vulgar jokes and profanity, Nate Bargatze announced his 2025 tour after the massive success of his 2024 The Be Funny Tour. It kicked off on March 19 in Las Vegas, almost immediately following smaller, local shows unrelated to his tour in Irvine, California, at the end of February and early March. His 2025 tour currently has over 100 stops scheduled, which includes additional time slots that have been added after the tour's initial announcement.

Here’s a breakdown of Nate Bargatze’s Big Dumb Eyes 2025 World Tour, including the tour schedule, purchasing details, as well as price comparisons between original tickets and verified resale tickets. You can also view show details and specifics at your own convenience on StubHub and Vivid Seats.

    How to buy tickets for Nate Bargatze’s 2025 comedy tour

    You can buy standard original tickets for Nate Bargatze’s Big Dumb Eyes World Tour on Ticketmaster. As of writing, the standard original tickets for most locations are either low in stock or entirely sold out. You can check Ticketmaster for the latest standard original ticket availability.

    Tickets can also be purchased from certified resale vendors such as StubHub and Vivid Seats. While many shows are currently sold out of standard original tickets, you can still find resale options for many of these locations available from these sites. For non-sold-out shows, there may also be more options in terms of seating availability and pricing options from these sites.

    It is also important to note that tickets for Nate Bargatze’s international stops are currently extremely limited. This is due to many international stops currently being sold out and resale options in high demand. You can check Stubhub, Vivid Seats, and Ticketmaster for the most current availability.

    How much are Nate Bargatze tickets?

    Tickets for Nate Bargatze’s 2025 Big Dumb Eyes Tour vary depending on the date, location, and demand for each show. For dates that still have tickets available on Ticketmaster, the original remaining ticket prices can be quite expensive. As most tour stops are sold out of original standard tickets, resale tickets on verified vendor sites such as StubHub or Vivid Seats may have more options, with ticket prices as low as $11.

    The lowest cost tickets for Nate Bargatze’s Big Dumb Eyes World Tour on StubHub range from $11 for his May 11 show in Fort Wayne to $483 in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia, on May 2. While there are a handful of shows over $200 for the cheapest available ticket, most Stubhub prices have decreased since the tour began with the lowest prices for most shows falling between $30 to $100 for his overall tour.

    Vivid Seats offers similar pricing to Stubhub, with the lowest-cost options ranging from $16 in Fort Wayne on May 11 to $309 in London on April 12, 2025. Like Stubhub, most resale ticket prices on Vivid Seats range from $30 to $100 before taxes and fees.

    There are currently no VIP packages on Ticketmaster for Nate Bargatze’s 2025 Big Dumb Eyes tour stops.

    Who is opening for Nate Bargatze’s tour?

    As of this writing, no openers have been confirmed for the 2025 Big Dumb Eyes Tour. During his previous 2024 The Be Funny Tour, Nate Bargatze had several different comedians as opening acts, including Graham Kay, Gary Vider, and Justin Smith. While it has not been announced who exactly will be opening for his 2025 shows, we can expect that there will be opening acts before Nate Bargatze hits the stage for the Big Dumb Eyes World Tour shows.

    Will there be international tour dates?

    Nate Bargatze’s Big Dumb Eyes World Tour has eight remaining international shows scheduled, including six stops in Europe and two in the Middle East.
