    • You can get your paycheck into your account up to two days early with several banks.
    • Other institutions make money by holding onto your paycheck longer to earn interest on the funds.
    • Early direct deposits give you quicker access to your money and may let you earn interest sooner.

    Direct deposit is the fastest way to get your paycheck into your checking account. Some of the best checking accounts make this process even faster by implementing early direct deposit features.

    Early direct deposit can effectively get your paycheck into your checking account up to two days early. Below, find 31 accounts from banks offering two-day early paychecks, an explanation of how they can do it, and why it can be smart to take advantage.

    Introduction to Early Direct Deposit Services

    Direct deposit is a service that lets you automatically put a payment into your savings account or checking account. Generally, direct deposits are used for paychecks, but they can also be used for things like government benefit payments or tax refunds. 

    When your employer initiates a direct deposit, it usually takes a couple of days for the money to go from your employer's account to your account. That's because direct deposits are a type of ACH transfer, a slow but inexpensive way to transfer money between bank accounts.

    With early direct deposits, your bank or credit union will automatically put money into your bank account as soon as it recognizes that a qualifying direct deposit has been initiated. You're essentially skipping the ACH transfer's waiting period.

    This can be helpful for making automatic bill payments on time without the need for credit card cash advances or other ways of accruing debt. Depending on payroll timing, it might also help you avoid overdraft fees.

    Eligibility and Sign-Up Process for Early Direct Deposit

    Qualifying for Early Direct Deposit

    Not all direct deposits are eligible for early direct deposit. For example, Capital One 360's website says that not every direct deposit is eligible for early direct deposit, with bank-to-bank transfer as an example of an ineligible direct deposit type. It also says that there's a transaction limit and monthly deposit dollar limit for early processing. Check with the financial institution you're interested in to see what requirements they have for early direct deposit.

    Keep in mind that, while savings accounts and checking accounts are both generally eligible for direct deposit, not every financial institution will offer early direct deposit for both types of accounts. If a bank that offers early direct deposit has a high-yield savings account you're interested in, make sure that this account specifically qualifies for early direct deposit.

    How to Sign Up for Early Direct Deposit

    You don't generally have to sign up for early direct deposit, as the best banks for early government benefit deposits and paycheck deposits automatically enroll you whenever you set up direct deposit for an eligible account. However, you will have to sign up for direct deposit with your employer to use early direct deposit. For that, you'll need at a minimum:

    • Your bank's name
    • The name the account is under
    • Your account number
    • Your routing number

    You can find your account number and routing number on checks issued by your bank. You might also need your address or the address of your bank. Talk to your employer to see what information they require for direct deposit.

    Maximizing Benefits of Early Direct Deposit

    Financial planning with early direct deposit banks makes paying bills an easier process. Frequently, bills are due on the same day your paycheck hits your account; if your paycheck arrives later in the day, you could end up with unpaid bills, a negative account balance, and overdraft fees. Early direct deposit delivers your paycheck early, so that doesn't happen.

    A frequent tip for learning how to budget is to have multiple bank accounts for different purposes. To maximize the benefits from early direct deposit, make sure the account that offers early direct deposit is the one that your paycheck is deposited in and your bills are paid through. Otherwise, the process of transferring your paycheck to the account that you pay bills from could eat up all of the extra time you gained from early direct deposit. 

    Not every bank account comes with early direct deposit, and even banks that do offer early direct deposit might not offer it for all of their bank accounts; for example, a bank might offer it for its standard checking account, but not for its interest-bearing checking account. If you are interested in that feature, you'll want to check with your financial institution to make sure it offers early direct deposit for the account you're interested in before committing.

    Customer Service and Early Direct Deposit

    Early direct deposit times vary on both the bank you're using and your employer. If you think you should be getting early direct deposit and you're not, or if you think you should be getting your paycheck earlier than you actually are, you might want to call your bank to see if you're correctly signed up for early direct deposit. Customer support will also be able to help you get an idea of the timeline you can expect for your direct deposit.

    If you're using an online bank, you should have digital access to customer service through phone lines or live chat. If you have a brick-and-mortar bank, you can go into a branch for customer support in addition to using phone lines and live chat.

    If you suspect you're not getting your early direct deposit paycheck because of fraud or unauthorized changes to your account, many banks offer 24/7 customer support lines for reporting fraud. If you do suspect fraud, make sure to call your bank or credit union as soon as possible.

    Bank Accounts With Early Direct Deposit FAQs

    How much earlier can I receive my pay with early direct deposit?

    Typically, the fastest banks can get your paycheck is one to two days earlier than you would otherwise.

    Are there any fees associated with using early direct deposit services?

    Almost all banks that offer early direct deposit are no-fee early salary deposit banks, at least in terms of using early direct deposit. Still, it's always important to check what fees a bank account charges before opening an account. 

    Is early direct deposit available for all types of payments?

    Generally, early direct deposit is used for paychecks. You might also be able to set up early direct deposit for things like government benefits; however, you'll want to check with your bank to confirm.

    Do I need to open a new account to use early direct deposit?

    If you're using an account that started offering early direct deposit after you opened your account, you can generally start using early direct deposit without opening a new account. If your bank account doesn't offer early direct deposit at all, you'll probably need to open a new account with a bank that does offer early direct deposit to use that feature.

    Is the money safe and insured when using early direct deposit?

    Yes. Money is usually safe because these features are usually connected to bank accounts. Funds received early are typically FDIC-insured and are as secure as your regular banking transactions.
